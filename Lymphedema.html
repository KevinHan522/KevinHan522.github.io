Project Description

The Kinect Lymphedema Detection project is a program with the goal of being able to detect and track lymphdema progress in patients. Lymphedema is a serious condition where the lymph nodes are unable to drain properly and thus limbs become inflated in serious cases. This program asks the patient to align themselves in front of the Kinect by placing red boxes on joints that are too far behind and then proceeds to measure the arm volume of both arms by first segmenting it from the other pixels via color segmentation and depth differences and then summing depths over all pixels in the arm. It then compares it with the other arm.

I worked on this project as the programming head for a full year from August 2014 to August 2015 at the Georgia Tech Laboratory of Lymphatic Biology and Bioengineering (LLBB) under Timothy Kassis and Mike Weiler who acted as clients. It was coded entirely in C# with the Microsoft Kinect v2 and Kinect SDK. Before working on the actual product, I was asked to demonstrate the feasibility of the idea by measuring the volume of a basketball, followed by a model arm, a full model, and eventually myself and others around the lab. We went on to various clients such as Emory and were able to generate interest in this product as it is easy to use and cheaper than the current alternatives. The prototype I made launched the idea into fruition and continues development with other interns as part of the startup LymphaTech.

Features

  • Color (and depth) segmentation
  • Volume Estimation
  • Multithreaded processing
  • Noise reduction by smoothing
